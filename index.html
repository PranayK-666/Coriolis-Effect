Coriolis Effect Simulation

Four cannons are fixed on a rotating disk. The red cannon can shoot a cannon ball towards the centre of the roundabout (use the "Shoot cannon" button) with an adjustable velocity. The left-hand animation shows the view from above, from a camera that is fixed in space relative to the ground. The right-hand animation shows the view from above, from a camera that is fixed to the roundabout.

The small red line on the red cannon shows the initial velocity vector of the cannon ball as it is released. In order to make the ball go directly through the centre of the roundabout, you will need to cancel out the tangential velocity: the red cannon needs to throw the ball towards his right since he is moving towards his left.

The cannon ball travels in a straight line through space (as you can see on the left-hand animation), but to observers who are rotating with the roundabout (the right-hand animation) it appears to travel through a curved path.
